    • B-cells & T-cells

    B-Cells and T-cells are apart of the lymphatic and immune system. They are lymphocytes (white blood cells). These white bllod cells are generateed in the thymus and bone marrow.


    B-cells

    B-cells produce antibodies that wipe out pathogens. They create these antibodies with the help of stimuli from T-cells due to a direct immune response.


    B-cells can also mutate into a blood cancer (leukemia and lymphoma). Regulatory B-cells supress T-cell responses, promoting cancer development.


    T-cells

    There are three different types of t-cells: cytotoxic t-cells, helper t-cells, and regulatory t-cells.


    -The cytotoxic (killer) T-cells help wipe out cancerous cells/infected cells.

    -The helper t-cells help stimulate the antibody production from B-cells and activate cytotoxic cells.

    -The regulatory t-cells help prevent autoimmune diseases. However, regulatory t-cells supress immune response by maintaining peripheral tolerance (second branch of immunological tolerance).
