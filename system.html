    • Lymphatic system


    The Lymphatic system is connected to the immune system and is made up of vessels, tissues, and nodes. The lymphatic organs also include the spleen and thymus.


    Function

    The lymphatic system collects excess lymph (the interstitial fluid) from cells and drains them into lymphatic capillaries (lymph vessels) to prevent the build-up of interstitial fluid in tissues.


    As the lymph circulates through the lymphatic vessels (which are all over the body), the lymph nodes- which are also apart of the immune system- filters out pathogens in the lymphatic fluid. These lymph nodes are able to filter out these substances because they contain lymphocytes (a type of white blood cell). After the lymph has been cleaned, it enters the blood through the right subclavian vein whcih is connected to the right lyphatic duct.
