    • Treatment for Lymphoma

    Treatment for Indolent Non Hodgkins Lymphoma

    Rituximab: Monoclonal antibody (the cloning of a unqiue white blood cells)


    Obinutuzmad: Anti-CD20 monoclonol antibody (used to acheieve B-cell (mutated cells) depletion)


    Lenalidomide and Rituximab: Combination of a cancer drug used to slow the growth of cancer cells (lenalidomide) and monoclonal antibodies (rituximab)


    Phosphatidylinostiol 3-Kinase (PI3K) Inhibitor: Blocks the PI3K pathway to slow down the growth of cancer


    E2H2 inhibitor (tazemetostat): prevents the growth of tumor


    Pallative raditation therapy: High energy rays are used to strink a cancer and slow it's growth


    Radiolabeled Anti-CD20 monoclonal antibodies: radiolabeled antibodies that have a specific target


    Chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T-cell therapy: These receptors bind to cancer cells and kills them


    Stem cell transplant: A transplant done to introduce healthier stem cells and replace the destroyed stem cells

    Treatment for Agressive Non Hodgkins Lymphoma

    Rituximab: Monoclonal antibody (the cloning of a unqiue white blood cells)

    Locanstiuximab testirine: antibody drug (CD-19) conjugate (antibody + chemically linked drug)


    Tafiasitamab plus lenalidomide: anti - CD19 monoclonial antibody (treat B-cell malignancies)


    Polatuzumab vedotin plus rituximab and bendamustine: antibody-drug conjugate targeting (D79b: a b-cell that has expressed 90% of B-cell mallignancies) plus an monoclonal antibody and a chemotherapy drug that prevents cancer cell growth


    Chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T-cell therapy: These receptors bind to cancer cells and kills them


    Stem cell transplant: A transplant done to introduce healthier stem cells and replace the destroyed stem cells


    Bone Marrow transplant: A transplant done to replace a bone marrow that isn't prodducing enough healthy blood cells.

    Treatment for Hodgkins Lymphoma

    Pembrolizumab: A humanized monoclonal antibody that blacks PD-1 allowing t-cells to kill cancer cells.


    Nivolumab: A human programmed death receptor-1 (PD-1) also known as a monoclonal antibody.

      - PD-1 will bound to a protein called PD-L1 (which keeps the immune system from attacking nonharmful cells). This increases the t-cells ability to kill cancer cells.


    Brentuximab vedotin: antibody-drug conjugate for certain CD-30 (Hodgkins lymphoma marker) lymphomas.


    Brentuximab vedotin + nivolumab: antibody-drug conjugate for certain CD-30 (Hodgkins lymphoma marker) lymphomas + A human programmed death receptor-1 (PD-1) also known as a monoclonal antibody.


    Chemotherapy regimen: Brentuximab, vedotin + doxorubicin (antibody-drug conjugate aganist CD-30), vinblastine (stops the growth of cancer cells), dacarbazine(stops the growth of cancer cells) )


    Radiation therapy: High doses of radiation used to kill cancer cells.


    Combination chemotherapy: The use of multiple chemotherapy drugs at one time (i.e. bleomycin, etoposide, and cisplatin).
