    • Non Hodgkins Lymphoma & Hodgkins Lymphoma

    Non Hodgkins Lymphoma (NHL) develops when a cell mutates in a lymph node or in any lymphatic sructure. 85-90 percent of the NHL cases start in the B-cells and a small percentage starts in T-cells. Non Hodgkins lymphoma are more common in adults rather than children. Moreover, when abnormal lymhocytes grow out of control it accumulates and forms masses. If this stays untreated, the cancerous cells will crowd normal white blood cells, weakening the immune system.


    Two different types of NHL:


    Agressive (fast spreading):

    - Anaplastic large-cell lymphoma (T-cell subtype lymphoma)

    - Burkitt lymphoma(Starts in the B-cells and grows rapidly)

    - Lymphoblastic lymphoma (Too many immature B-cells)

    - Mantle cell lymphoma (B-lymphoctes in the mantle zone grow uncontrollably)

    - Peripheral T-cell lymphoma (Developed from T-cells and shows in skin forms away from the center of the body)


    Indolent (slow spreading):

    - Folicular Lymphoma (Body making abnormal B-cells)

    - Cutanrous T-cell lymphoma (Begins in T-cells when T-cells developp abnormalities that attack the skin)

    - Lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma (Mature B-cells)

    - MALT & Marginal zone B-cell Lymphoma (Mucosaassociated (inner linings of organs) lymphoid tissue and originates from b-cells)


    Hodgkins Lymphoma (Hodgkins disease) develops when lymphocytes grow out of control, causing lymph nodes to swell.


    What are B Symptoms?

    B symptoms refer to fever, night sweats, and weightloss.


    Different types of Hodgkin's Lymphoma:


    - Nodular sclerosis Hodgkin's lymphoma (MOST COMMON):

    Involved lymph nodes containing fibrous tissues (sclerosis) and causes B symptoms


    - Mixed cellularity Hodgkin's lymphoma (SECOND MOST COMMON):

    involved Reed- Sternberg cells (Large, abnormal Lymphocytes) and affects lymph nodes in the abdomen and spleen & causes B symptoms


    - Lymphocyte depleted Hodgkin's lymphoma (RARE):

    affects lymph nodes in the chest, abdomen, spleen, liver and bone marrow


    - Lymphocyte-rich Hodgkin's lymphoma (RARE):

    develops in lymph nodes in the neck, armpits, and collarbone; don't cause B symptoms
